Welcome to Semporna

Semporna is a coastal town located in the eastern part of Sabah, Malaysia, on the island of Borneo. Known as the gateway to the world-famous Sipadan Island, Semporna is a popular destination for divers and snorkelers seeking to explore some of the most vibrant coral reefs and marine life in the world. The town itself is a bustling hub with a rich cultural mix of Bajau, Suluk, and other indigenous communities, who are known for their seafaring traditions. With its crystal-clear waters, stunning marine biodiversity, and picturesque islands, Semporna offers a perfect blend of adventure and relaxation for travelers.

The best time to visit Semporna is during the dry season, from March to October. This period offers the best visibility for diving and snorkeling, with calm seas and pleasant weather.

April to June is peak season for divers, especially for those heading to Sipadan Island, so plan ahead and book your accommodation and permits early.

Transport Recommendations

  • By Air: The nearest airport to Semporna is Tawau Airport (TWU), located about 1.5 hours away by car. You can fly into Tawau from major cities like Kuala Lumpur or Kota Kinabalu. From the airport, you can take a taxi or arrange a shuttle to Semporna.
  • By Bus: If you’re already in Sabah, you can take a bus from Kota Kinabalu or Sandakan to Semporna. The journey is long, taking around 8-10 hours from Kota Kinabalu.

Activities You Must Do

  • Diving: Semporna is a gateway to some of the world’s best dive sites, including Sipadan Island, Mabul Island, and Kapalai. If you’re diving in Sipadan, make sure to secure a permit in advance, as only 120 permits are issued per day.
  • Snorkeling: If you’re not a diver, snorkeling is a great way to experience the vibrant marine life. Many tour operators offer snorkeling trips to nearby islands.
  • Island Hopping: Explore the stunning islands around Semporna, such as Bohey Dulang, Mataking, and Pom Pom. Each island offers unique landscapes, from volcanic craters to white sandy beaches.

Important Notices

If you plan to dive, ensure you have a recognized diving certification. If you’re a beginner, consider taking a dive course at one of the local dive centers.
