Welcome to Mari-Mari Cultural Village

Mari-Mari Cultural Village is a unique cultural attraction located near Kota Kinabalu, Sabah, Malaysia. The village offers an immersive experience into the traditional lifestyles of five indigenous ethnic groups of Sabah: the Kadazan-Dusun, Bajau, Murut, Rungus, and Lundayeh. Visitors can explore authentic replicas of traditional houses, witness cultural performances, and learn about the various customs, crafts, and rituals practiced by these communities. Mari-Mari Cultural Village provides a fascinating insight into the rich cultural heritage of Sabah, making it a must-visit for those interested in understanding the diverse cultures of Borneo.

The village offers guided tours three times a day—typically in the morning, mid-day, and afternoon. It’s best to book your tour in advance to secure your preferred time slot.

Interactive Experience

You’ll have the opportunity to participate in activities such as blowpipe shooting, rice wine tasting, and fire-starting demonstrations. Engaging with these activities enhances the experience.

Health and Safety

  • Stay Hydrated: The tropical climate can be hot and humid, so drink plenty of water during your visit.
  • Follow Guidelines: Always follow the instructions provided by the guides, especially during interactive activities, to ensure your safety and the respect of cultural practices.

Be Aware

Photography is generally allowed, but it’s polite to ask before taking close-up shots of individuals or sensitive cultural practices. Avoid using flash photography during performances or in dimly lit areas, as it can be disruptive.
