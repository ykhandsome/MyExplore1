Welcome to Kuala Gandah Elephant Sanctuary

Kuala Gandah Elephant Sanctuary is a renowned conservation center located in the state of Pahang, Malaysia. Established in 1989, the sanctuary is dedicated to the rescue, rehabilitation, and relocation of endangered Asian elephants. Visitors to the sanctuary can learn about the challenges facing wild elephants in Malaysia, witness the care given to these majestic animals, and even participate in feeding or bathing them under the guidance of the sanctuary staff. The Kuala Gandah Elephant Sanctuary plays a crucial role in wildlife conservation and offers a unique, educational experience for those interested in wildlife and nature.

Visiting Hours: The sanctuary is open daily from 10:00 AM to 4:30 PM. Arrive early to avoid crowds and get the most out of your visit.

Transport Recommendations

  • By Car: The sanctuary is located about 2 hours from Kuala Lumpur by car. The drive is straightforward, and parking is available on-site. Consider renting a car for flexibility.
  • By Tour: Many tour operators in Kuala Lumpur offer day trips to Kuala Gandah, often combined with other nearby attractions like Deerland Park. This is a convenient option if you prefer not to drive.

Activities Must Do

Elephant Activities: Visitors can watch elephants being bathed, fed, and cared for by the staff. Depending on the schedule, you may also have the opportunity to feed or bathe the elephants.

Safety Tips

  • Always follow the instructions of the sanctuary staff when interacting with the elephants. Keep a safe distance from the animals unless invited to interact by the guides.
  • While photography is allowed, avoid using flash as it can startle the elephants.

Tickets and Donations

  • The sanctuary operates on a donation basis, and visitors are encouraged to contribute to support the conservation efforts. Donations can be made at the entrance.
  • While you can visit without a reservation, it’s a good idea to book in advance, especially if you’re visiting as part of a group.
