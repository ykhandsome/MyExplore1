Welcome to Pulau Jejerak

Pulau Jerejak is a small island located off the southeastern coast of Penang, Malaysia. Often referred to as the "Alcatraz of Malaysia," the island has a rich history, having served as a leper colony, a quarantine station, and a penal colony during the British colonial era. Today, Pulau Jerejak is a popular destination for its natural beauty and recreational activities, including hiking, cycling, and water sports. The island is also home to a resort that offers visitors a peaceful retreat away from the bustling city of Penang, making it an attractive spot for both history enthusiasts and nature lovers.

The best time to visit Pulau Jerejak is during the dry season, from November to March, when the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities. Avoid the monsoon season (May to October), as heavy rains can disrupt travel and activities.

Accommodation

  • Resort Stay: Pulau Jerejak is home to Jerejak Island Resort, which offers various accommodations ranging from standard rooms to more luxurious options. Staying at the resort allows you to fully enjoy the island’s amenities and activities.
  • Day Trip: If you’re not staying overnight, you can still explore the island on a day trip. Make sure to plan your return ferry accordingly.

Activities

  • Water Sports: The island’s calm waters are perfect for kayaking, paddleboarding, and swimming. Some resorts offer equipment rentals for these activities.
  • Relaxation: Enjoy the serene beaches and take in the tranquil atmosphere. The resort’s spa facilities are also available for those looking to unwind.

Important Notice

  • Use insect repellent to protect against mosquitoes, especially if you’re exploring the island’s forests.
  • The tropical sun can be intense, so apply sunscreen regularly and wear protective clothing.
