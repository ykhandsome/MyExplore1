Welcome to Bukit Larut

Bukit Larut, also known as Maxwell Hill, is a serene hill station located in Perak, Malaysia. It is the oldest hill station in the country, established during the British colonial era. Situated at an elevation of about 1,250 meters above sea level, Bukit Larut offers cool temperatures, lush rainforests, and panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. The area is known for its tranquil environment, diverse flora and fauna, and pleasant climate, making it a popular destination for nature enthusiasts and those seeking a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of city life. The hill station features a range of activities, including hiking, nature walks, and scenic rides in the vintage Land Rovers that ascend the hill.

Bukit Larut is known for its cool climate, with temperatures often ranging between 15°C to 25°C. It’s pleasant year-round, but the best time to visit is during the dry season, from March to October, to avoid the heavy rains of the monsoon season.

Transport Recommendations

  • By Car: Bukit Larut is about a 30-minute drive from Taiping, Perak. The journey involves a winding road, so ensure your vehicle is in good condition. There is parking available at the base of the hill.
  • By Taxi: You can take a taxi from Taiping to the base of Bukit Larut. Arrange the return trip in advance if possible.

Activities Recommendations

If you enjoy hiking, there are trails available for different skill levels. The walk from the base to the summit takes about 1.5 to 2 hours, offering beautiful views along the way.

Nearby Attractions

  • After visiting Bukit Larut, explore Taiping, known for its historic sites, including the Taiping Lake Gardens, Perak Museum, and the Taiping Zoo.
  • A visit to the nearby fishing village of Kuala Sepetang offers a chance to see mangrove forests and enjoy fresh seafood.

Important Notices

  • Bukit Larut is a natural area, so be respectful of the environment. Avoid littering and stick to marked trails.
  • Observe wildlife from a distance and do not feed or disturb animals.
