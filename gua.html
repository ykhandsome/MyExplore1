Welcome to Gua Niah

Gua Niah, or Niah Cave, is a famous archaeological and natural site located in Niah National Park in Sarawak, Malaysia. Known for its immense historical significance, Gua Niah is home to some of the oldest human remains in Southeast Asia, dating back over 40,000 years. The cave system features massive caverns, ancient rock paintings, and artifacts that offer a glimpse into prehistoric life. In addition to its archaeological importance, Gua Niah is also known for its impressive limestone formations and the diverse wildlife that inhabits the surrounding rainforest, making it a popular destination for both history enthusiasts and nature lovers.

The best time to visit Gua Niah is during the dry season, from March to September, when the weather is more conducive for trekking and cave exploration. The trails can be slippery during the rainy season. Arrive early in the day to avoid the heat and to have more time to explore the caves and surrounding areas.

Activities Recommended

  • Cave Exploration: Gua Niah is part of a larger cave system, including the Great Cave and the Painted Cave. Expect a mix of history and nature as you explore these ancient sites, with highlights including prehistoric rock paintings and the famous burial sites.
  • Trekking: The trek to the caves involves a 3.5 km walk through the rainforest, which takes about an hour. The trail is well-marked but can be challenging, especially in humid conditions.
  • Wildlife: Keep an eye out for the diverse wildlife in the area, including birds, insects, and occasionally monkeys. The caves are also home to bats and swiftlets.

Tickets and Entrance Fees

  • There is an entrance fee for Niah National Park, which includes access to Gua Niah and other attractions in the park. Tickets can be purchased at the park headquarters.
  • You’ll need to take a short boat ride across the Niah River to reach the trailhead for the caves. This service is provided by the park for a small fee.

Nearby Attractions

After visiting Gua Niah, consider exploring Miri, known for its beaches, national parks, and vibrant local markets.

Located closer to Miri, this park offers additional trekking opportunities and waterfalls for those interested in more outdoor adventures.

Important Notices

  • Gua Niah is a site of great historical and cultural significance, so treat it with respect. Do not touch or damage the ancient rock paintings or artifacts.
  • Follow the marked trails and heed any safety warnings provided by park guides. Be cautious when exploring the caves, as some areas have steep drops and slippery surfaces.
