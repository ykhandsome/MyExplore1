Welcome to Kundasang

Kundasang is a picturesque town nestled in the highlands of Sabah, Malaysia, just a short distance from Mount Kinabalu, Southeast Asia's highest peak. Known for its cool climate and stunning landscapes, Kundasang is often referred to as the "New Zealand of Borneo" due to its rolling green hills and dairy farms. The town is a popular destination for nature lovers and adventurers, offering breathtaking views, lush vegetable farms, and vibrant local markets. Kundasang also holds historical significance, being near the site of the Kundasang War Memorial, which honors the soldiers who lost their lives during World War II.

Kundasang enjoys a cool, temperate climate year-round, but the best time to visit is during the dry season, from March to September, when the weather is clearer for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Transport Recommendations

  • By Car: Kundasang is about a 2-hour drive from Kota Kinabalu, the capital of Sabah. Renting a car gives you the flexibility to explore at your own pace. The road to Kundasang is winding but well-paved, offering beautiful views along the way.
  • By Bus or Van: Public minibuses or shared vans (locally known as "bas mini") operate between Kota Kinabalu and Kundasang. These are more budget-friendly but may be less comfortable and less flexible in terms of schedule.

Activities and Attractions

  • Mount Kinabalu: If you’re up for a challenge, consider climbing Mount Kinabalu, one of Southeast Asia’s highest peaks. Permits and guides are required, and it’s best to book your climb months in advance.
  • Desa Dairy Farm: Visit the Desa Dairy Farm, often called the “Little New Zealand” of Sabah, for a taste of fresh milk and dairy products, and to enjoy the picturesque scenery of rolling hills and grazing cows.
  • Poring Hot Springs: Located about 40 minutes from Kundasang, Poring Hot Springs offers a relaxing soak in natural hot springs. Nearby, you can explore the Poring Canopy Walk and Kipungit Waterfall.

Important Notices

Kundasang can get crowded during Malaysian public holidays and school vacations, so plan your trip during off-peak times for a more peaceful experience.

Kundasang is home to the indigenous Dusun people. Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting villages or cultural sites.
