Welcome to Kenyir Lake

Kenyir Lake is a vast man-made reservoir located in Terengganu, Malaysia, near the border with Kelantan. Covering an area of approximately 260,000 hectares, it is one of the largest artificial lakes in Southeast Asia. Created by the construction of the Kenyir Dam, the lake is surrounded by lush rainforest and is known for its scenic beauty, with numerous islands, waterfalls, and rivers dotting the landscape. Kenyir Lake is a popular destination for eco-tourism and recreational activities such as boating, fishing, and exploring the surrounding wildlife. The area is also home to diverse flora and fauna, making it a haven for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers.

The best time to visit Kenyir Lake is during the dry season, from March to September. This period offers clearer weather and better conditions for outdoor activities.

Transport Recommendations

  • By Car: Kenyir Lake is accessible by car from Kuala Terengganu, which is about a 2-hour drive away. The drive is relatively straightforward, but be prepared for winding roads as you approach the lake.
  • By Bus: You can take a bus from Kuala Lumpur or other major cities to Kuala Terengganu, and then hire a taxi or arrange a transfer to Kenyir Lake.

Activities

  • Boating: Explore the lake by renting a boat or joining a boat tour. This is a great way to see the lake’s islands, waterfalls, and hidden spots.
  • Fishing: Kenyir Lake is popular for fishing. Bring your own equipment or rent from local operators.
  • Waterfalls: Visit some of the lake’s beautiful waterfalls, such as Lasir Falls and Tanjung Lesung. These are accessible by boat and hiking.

Important Notice

Mobile coverage might be limited in some areas around the lake. It’s a good idea to inform someone of your plans before heading out.
