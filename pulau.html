Welcome to Pulau Sembilan

Pulau Sembilan is a picturesque cluster of nine small islands located off the coast of Perak, Malaysia. Known for its crystal-clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and diverse marine life, Pulau Sembilan is a popular destination for snorkeling, diving, and fishing enthusiasts. The islands are also famous for the "Blue Tears" phenomenon, where bioluminescent plankton lights up the shoreline at night, creating a magical glow. Though relatively less developed and remote, Pulau Sembilan offers visitors a tranquil escape, surrounded by pristine nature and stunning seascapes.

The best time to visit Pulau Sembilan is during the dry season, from March to September. The weather is more predictable, and the seas are calmer, making it ideal for water activities.

Transport Recommendations

  • By Boat: Pulau Sembilan is accessible only by boat. The main departure point is the town of Lumut in Perak. You can take a boat or ferry from Lumut Jetty, and the journey to the islands takes about 45 minutes to an hour.
  • Boat Transfers: Some travel agencies and tour operators offer package deals that include boat transfers, meals, and activities. It's a good idea to book in advance, especially during peak seasons.

Must-Do Activities

  • Blue Tears Phenomenon: If you stay overnight, don't miss the chance to witness the "Blue Tears" phenomenon, where bioluminescent plankton creates a glowing effect in the water at night.

Environmental Responsibility

Pulau Sembilan is a natural paradise, so it's important to protect its environment. Avoid leaving any trash behind, and respect the local wildlife and coral reefs. When snorkeling or diving, avoid touching or stepping on the coral reefs, as they are fragile and take a long time to grow.
