Welcome to Astaka Morocco

Astaka Morocco, also known as the Moroccan Pavilion, is a stunning architectural landmark located in the Putrajaya Botanical Garden, Malaysia. This exquisite structure was built to symbolize the diplomatic ties between Malaysia and Morocco, reflecting the rich cultural heritage and artistic traditions of Morocco. The pavilion features intricate Moroccan designs, including ornate tile work, carved wooden doors, and beautiful courtyards, all inspired by the palaces and gardens of Morocco. Astaka Morocco serves as a peaceful retreat and a unique cultural attraction, offering visitors a taste of Moroccan art and architecture in the heart of Malaysia.

Visit in the morning or late afternoon to avoid the midday heat, as Putrajaya can get quite warm. These times also offer softer lighting, which is ideal for photography.

Exploring the Area

  • Botanical Garden: After visiting Astaka Morocco, take some time to explore the Putrajaya Botanical Garden. The garden is expansive, with various themed sections, walking paths, and lakes, making it a perfect place for a relaxing stroll.
  • Picnic: The garden is also an ideal spot for a picnic, with plenty of shaded areas where you can relax and enjoy the scenery.

Cultural Sensitivity

  • Astaka Morocco is a cultural landmark, so be respectful of the property and the cultural significance it represents. Avoid touching or damaging the intricate decorations.
  • While photography is encouraged, be mindful of other visitors and avoid obstructing paths or areas with your equipment.

Local Amenities

  • Facilities are available within the Putrajaya Botanical Garden. It’s a good idea to use them before heading to Astaka Morocco, as the pavilion itself does not have restrooms.
  • There are cafes and eateries nearby where you can grab a snack or meal. Alternatively, bring your own refreshments for a picnic in the garden.
